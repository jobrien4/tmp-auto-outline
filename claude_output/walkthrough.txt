AutoCDP Version 3 — System Walkthrough
========================================
For Executive and Non-Technical Stakeholders


WHAT CHANGED AND WHY IT MATTERS
-------------------------------

Versions 1 and 2 proved that turning messy dealership data into
targeted, legally compliant direct mail campaigns delivers measurable
return on investment. Version 1 handled a handful of pilot dealers.
Version 2 automated the data flow with nightly updates and gave
dealers a self-service dashboard to approve campaigns.

Version 3 is the leap from "proven tool" to "self-improving autonomous
marketing engine." Three fundamental capabilities change the economics
of the platform:

  1. The system now gets smarter on its own — every month.
  2. It picks the cheapest effective way to reach each customer.
  3. A single executive dashboard spans every dealership in a group.


HOW THE SYSTEM NOW LEARNS ON ITS OWN
-------------------------------------

In Versions 1 and 2, the predictive scoring model — the mathematical
engine that decides which customers are most likely to buy — was
trained once and frozen. It worked, but it could not adapt. If buying
patterns shifted (say, more customers began responding to text messages
instead of mail), the model had no way to absorb that signal.

Version 3 introduces an Automated Learning Loop. Every month, the
system examines the actual outcomes of every campaign it has run
across every dealership. It looks at who received an offer, through
which channel, and whether they ultimately purchased a vehicle. It
then retrains the prediction model on this ever-growing body of real
results. When the new model proves more accurate than the old one in
controlled testing, it automatically replaces the old model in
production.

In concrete terms: if the system discovers that customers with positive
equity and two or more recent service visits convert at 12 percent
when contacted by text message, it will learn that pattern and begin
scoring those customers higher — without any human analyst needing to
spot the trend. The more data flows through the system, the sharper
the predictions become. This is the flywheel effect: more dealers
generate more data, which trains better models, which produce higher
conversion rates, which attract more dealers.


SMART CHANNEL ROUTING — WHERE MARGINS IMPROVE
----------------------------------------------

Versions 1 and 2 only used printed mail. A single mail piece costs
roughly one dollar to print, address, and post. Version 3 adds two
new channels: text messaging at roughly one cent per message, and
email at roughly one-tenth of a cent per message.

The Smart Channel Router is the decision layer that sits between the
scoring engine and the delivery channels. For each customer flagged
as likely to buy, the router evaluates:

  - Is this customer currently in a rest period on any channel? (We
    enforce mandatory cooling-off windows: 45 days for mail, 7 days
    for text, 14 days for email. No customer is over-contacted.)
  - Which open channels are available?
  - What is the estimated conversion rate for each open channel, given
    this customer's profile?
  - What does each channel cost?

The router then selects the cheapest channel that still meets the
target conversion rate. If a customer responds well to text messages,
there is no reason to spend a dollar on a mail piece when a one-cent
text message will do the job. This is where margins expand
dramatically.

Consider the math for 500 dealerships, each running 10,000 customer
contacts per month:

  All mail:    5,000,000 pieces x $1.00 = $5,000,000 in channel cost
  Smart mix:   1,000,000 mail + 3,000,000 SMS + 1,000,000 email
               = $1,000,000 + $30,000 + $1,000 = $1,031,000

That is roughly a four-million-dollar monthly reduction in channel
costs across the platform — money that stays as margin or can be
passed back to dealers as lower campaign pricing.

The key insight: we are not replacing mail with cheaper channels
blindly. The router only shifts a customer to text or email when the
data supports that the conversion rate will hold. Mail remains the
default for segments where print outperforms digital.


THE ENTERPRISE DATA WAREHOUSE
------------------------------

Versions 1 and 2 kept each dealership's data in a separate
compartment within our main transaction database. That design is
excellent for data isolation and privacy. But it makes cross-dealer
analytics slow. When a dealer group CEO wants to see total campaign
performance across 50 stores, the system had to query 50 separate
compartments and stitch the results together.

Version 3 solves this by adding a dedicated Enterprise Data Warehouse.
As data flows through the transaction database, changes are
automatically captured and streamed into a central data lake organized
by dealership. The warehouse sits on top of that lake and can answer
complex questions — "Show me cost per conversion by channel across all
50 stores for the last 90 days" — in under one second, even across
hundreds of millions of records.

The transaction database continues to handle the daily operational
work: syncing data, scoring customers, dispatching campaigns. The
warehouse handles the heavy analytical work: executive dashboards,
trend analysis, model accuracy tracking, and channel performance
comparisons. Neither system slows the other down.


WHAT THE CEO DASHBOARD SHOWS
-----------------------------

A dealer group executive logging into the Version 3 dashboard sees:

  - Group Overview: Total campaigns sent, total responses, total
    sales attributed, overall return on marketing investment — across
    every dealership in the group.

  - Channel Performance: Side-by-side comparison of mail, text, and
    email — cost per contact, response rate, conversion rate, and
    cost per sale — by dealership and by time period.

  - Model Accuracy: How well the predictive engine is performing. Are
    the customers it scores highest actually buying at the predicted
    rates? Is the model improving month over month?

  - Per-Dealer Comparison: Rank dealerships by campaign volume,
    conversion rate, cost efficiency, and revenue generated.

All of these views load in under one second, regardless of how many
dealerships or how many millions of records are involved, because
they are powered by the Enterprise Data Warehouse rather than the
operational database.


COMPLIANCE — UNCHANGED AND NON-NEGOTIABLE
-------------------------------------------

Every compliance safeguard from Versions 1 and 2 remains fully in
place. The Compliance Firewall — the deterministic math engine that
independently verifies every financial figure before it reaches a
customer — now operates across all three channels. A text message
containing an offer gets the same federal Regulation Z scrutiny as a
printed letter. The compliance templates are adapted per channel: a
160-character text message requires different formatting than a full
printed letter, but the mathematical verification is identical. Every
check, pass or fail, continues to be recorded in the immutable audit
ledger.


COST STRUCTURE AT SCALE
------------------------

Platform Infrastructure:     $8,000 - $30,000 per month
  This covers all cloud services: the transaction database, the data
  warehouse, the automated learning loop, the channel router, and all
  compute for processing and dispatch. The range depends on the number
  of active dealerships (50 to 500).

AI Writing Costs:            $5,000 - $20,000 per month
  Generating personalized offers across three channels for up to 500
  dealerships. Text messages are shorter and cheaper to generate than
  full print letters.

Channel Costs (pass-through to dealer budgets):
  Print:  ~$1.00 per piece
  Text:   ~$0.01 per message
  Email:  ~$0.001 per message

At the target scale of 500 dealerships generating $18,000 per dealer
per month in revenue, the platform produces $9 million per month in
gross revenue against roughly $50,000 per month in platform operating
costs — before channel pass-through. Gross margins on the platform
itself remain above 90 percent.

Smart channel routing further improves the unit economics for dealers.
As the system shifts eligible contacts from dollar-per-piece mail to
penny-per-message text, the dealer's effective cost per conversion
drops, making the service more valuable and increasing retention.


THE BIGGER PICTURE
-------------------

Version 3 is where AutoCDP transitions from a campaign execution tool
into a self-improving autonomous marketing engine. The learning loop
ensures the system gets sharper with every cycle. The channel router
ensures every dollar of marketing spend is deployed as efficiently as
possible. The enterprise warehouse gives group executives the
real-time visibility they need to manage marketing across dozens or
hundreds of stores.

Each new dealership that joins the platform contributes data that
makes the models better for every other dealership. This network
effect is the strategic moat: the larger the platform grows, the
harder it becomes for a competitor to match the prediction accuracy
and channel optimization that AutoCDP delivers.

Version 3 supports 50 to 500 dealerships and $9 million to $90
million in annual recurring revenue. It is the foundation for
everything that follows.
