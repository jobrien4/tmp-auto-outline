AutoCDP Version 1 -- Platform Walkthrough
==========================================
For Executive and Non-Technical Stakeholders


WHAT AUTOCDP IS AND WHY IT EXISTS
----------------------------------

AutoCDP is an autonomous marketing platform built specifically for
automobile dealerships. Its job is to look at a dealership's existing
customer data, figure out which customers are most likely to buy a car
in the next 60 to 90 days, write them a personalized, legally compliant
financial offer, and mail it to their home -- then track exactly which
customers responded and drove back to the lot.

The problem it solves is real and expensive. Most dealerships have
thousands of past customers sitting in their computer systems whose
ownership timelines, equity positions, and service histories are
screaming "ready to buy" -- but nobody has the time or the tools to
identify them, let alone write a personal offer for each one and send
it at the right moment. Dealers either blast generic mailers to
everyone (expensive, low return) or rely on salespeople to manually
work old leads (inconsistent, time-consuming). AutoCDP replaces both
of those approaches with a fully automated, data-driven process.

Version 1 is the pilot. It serves one to five dealerships, proves the
core prediction-plus-print loop works, and generates enough real-world
results to validate the economics before any investment in scale.


STEP ONE: THE DEALER UPLOADS THEIR DATA
----------------------------------------

AutoCDP never connects directly into a dealership's internal computer
system. There are no software agents installed at the dealership, no
live API connections, and no ongoing read access to the dealer's
network. This is intentional. It eliminates a large category of
security and liability risk, and it means the platform works with any
dealership system that can export a CSV file -- which is all of them.

The process is simple. An AutoCDP administrator generates a secure,
time-limited upload link (valid for a short window, tied to that
specific dealership's storage area). A dealership staff member uses
that link to upload their customer export file -- typically a
spreadsheet exported from their CRM system containing customer names,
contact information, vehicle ownership records, and service history.
The file goes directly into AutoCDP's Secure Data Vault (a
private cloud storage area) without passing through any intermediate
server.

Once the upload completes, the platform automatically detects the new
file and begins processing it. No human at AutoCDP needs to manually
kick off the next step.


STEP TWO: CLEANING THE DATA
-----------------------------

Dealership CRM exports are almost always messy. A customer might
appear three times with slight variations in their name or address.
Phone numbers may include or exclude dashes. Email addresses may be
duplicated across records. Addresses may be incomplete or formatted
inconsistently.

AutoCDP's Data Cleaning Engine reads the uploaded file and runs it
through a multi-stage scrubbing process. It standardizes name formats,
normalizes phone and address fields, identifies and merges duplicate
records (a customer who appears twice becomes a single clean record
with both records' data consolidated), and flags rows that are too
incomplete to be useful.

The output of this stage is what AutoCDP calls a Golden Record for
each unique customer: one clean, complete, deduplicated entry in the
Secure Data Vault that represents everything the system knows about
that person from across all the messy source rows.

This cleaning step matters more than it might seem. The Predictive
Math Engine that comes next is only as accurate as the data it reads.
Duplicate records skew the predictions. Malformed addresses cause
mail to be returned undelivered. Getting this stage right is what
separates a reliable system from an expensive mistake.


STEP THREE: IDENTIFYING LIKELY BUYERS
---------------------------------------

Once the customer data is clean, AutoCDP's Predictive Math Engine
scores every customer record. The engine is a mathematical model
trained on real automotive retail conversion data. It looks at a
combination of signals for each customer:

  - How long ago did they purchase or lease their current vehicle?
  - If they are leasing, when does their lease end?
  - Does the current value of their vehicle exceed what they owe on it
    (positive equity)? By how much?
  - How recently have they come in for service?
  - How many service visits have they had in the past year?

The engine combines these signals into a single number between 0 and 1
for each customer -- their purchase likelihood score. A score of 0.90
means the model believes there is a very high probability this customer
is in a buying window. A score of 0.20 means the model sees few
signals of near-term purchase intent.

Every score is written to the Secure Data Vault alongside the specific
signals that produced it, so the reasoning is always auditable.

For Version 1, the Predictive Math Engine uses a pre-trained model --
meaning the mathematical weights were computed in advance by our data
science team using historical automotive retail data. The model is
stable and does not update itself during Version 1 operations. That
automatic learning capability is built into later versions.


STEP FOUR: CHECKING THE DO-NOT-CONTACT LIST
---------------------------------------------

Before any customer is selected for a campaign, the platform checks
two things.

First, has this customer already received mail from this dealership
recently? AutoCDP maintains a mandatory rest period of 45 days between
mail contacts per customer. This is not configurable. A customer who
received a mailer 20 days ago is automatically skipped, regardless of
their prediction score. This prevents spam, protects the dealership's
brand, and keeps AutoCDP off postal blacklists.

Second, was this customer previously flagged as a do-not-contact? Any
customer who has opted out is permanently excluded from all future
campaigns without exception.

Only customers who pass both checks AND have a prediction score above
the configured threshold (default: 0.70) move forward to the offer
generation step.


STEP FIVE: WRITING THE OFFER
------------------------------

For each customer who passes the selection step, AutoCDP generates a
personalized financial offer. This is where the AI writing system
comes in.

The AI writing system takes the customer's profile -- their vehicle,
equity position, current payment, and projected new payment -- and
composes a letter addressed to that specific person with specific
numbers. A customer with $4,200 in positive equity leasing a 2022
Honda CR-V gets a different letter than a customer who owns a 2019
Ford F-150 outright. The offers are genuinely personalized, not just
mail-merge templates.

However, because these letters contain financial terms -- Annual
Percentage Rate, monthly payment, loan term -- they are subject to
federal law. Under the Truth-in-Lending Act (also known as Regulation
Z), financial disclosures in any customer communication must meet
specific accuracy and formatting requirements. Violations carry real
legal liability for the dealership.

AutoCDP addresses this with a mandatory Compliance Firewall. The AI
writing system is never allowed to send copy directly to a customer.
Every piece of generated copy is intercepted by a separate,
deterministic rules engine that independently re-computes the APR,
monthly payment, and total cost of financing from first principles --
using the same inputs the AI used. The rules engine then compares its
calculations to what the AI wrote.

If the numbers match within acceptable tolerance and all required
disclosures are present in the correct format: the copy is approved
and moves forward.

If any number is wrong, any required disclosure is missing, or any
field fails a format check: the copy is rejected. The rejection is
logged in detail, including exactly which check failed and what values
were involved. The campaign record is flagged as compliance-failed and
goes no further. No non-compliant offer ever reaches a customer.

Every compliance decision -- pass or fail -- is recorded in a
permanent, append-only audit log that cannot be modified after the
fact. This log is available for regulatory review at any time.


STEP SIX: SENDING THE MAIL
----------------------------

Once an offer clears the Compliance Firewall, AutoCDP submits it to
the print-and-mail partner (Lob.com). Lob prints the letter, places
it in an addressed envelope, and delivers it through standard postal
mail. Lob returns a tracking identifier that AutoCDP stores alongside
the campaign record.

The letter includes a unique QR code printed directly on it. That QR
code is specific to this campaign and this customer. When the customer
scans it, the scan is recorded and the customer is redirected to the
dealership's inventory page.

The entire step -- submitting the offer, receiving the tracking
confirmation, and updating the campaign record -- is automated.


STEP SEVEN: TRACKING THE RESULT
---------------------------------

When a customer scans their QR code, AutoCDP's Tracking Service
receives the request, logs it (including when it happened), and
immediately sends the customer to the destination URL. The scan event
is linked back to the specific campaign record and the specific
customer who received the mailer.

This creates a clean, auditable chain: dealer CSV upload -> clean
record -> prediction score -> offer generated -> offer approved ->
mailer sent -> QR scanned -> customer arrived at inventory. Every step
is timestamped and logged.

Dealership staff can view campaign performance through the analytics
dashboard at any time:

  - How many customers were scored above threshold this run?
  - How many passed cooldown checks?
  - How many offers cleared compliance?
  - How many mailers were dispatched?
  - How many QR codes have been scanned?
  - How many scans have been converted to sales (manually attributed)?

This gives both the dealership and the AutoCDP team full visibility
into where customers are in the funnel and what the campaign is
producing.


WHAT IS NOT IN VERSION 1
--------------------------

Version 1 is deliberately scoped. The following capabilities are not
present in Version 1 and are planned for later versions:

  - Automatic data syncs from dealership systems (Version 2 adds this
    via third-party aggregators; Version 1 requires manual CSV upload)
  - Digital channels: text message and email (Version 3)
  - Automated model retraining on real results (Version 3)
  - Self-service dealer onboarding portal (Version 2)
  - Multi-store group dashboards spanning many dealerships (Version 3)

Version 1 is operated with AutoCDP staff assistance. Dealer upload,
pipeline monitoring, and analytics review are handled through direct
communication with the AutoCDP team.


WHAT VERSION 1 COSTS AND WHAT IT PROVES
-----------------------------------------

Cloud infrastructure for Version 1 runs approximately $300 to $700
per month regardless of campaign volume -- well within pilot economics.
Print-and-mail costs are approximately $1.00 per piece and are passed
through directly to the dealer's campaign budget. A dealership running
5,000 to 10,000 mailers per month pays that cost directly to the mail
partner; it does not pass through AutoCDP's margins.

The Version 1 commercial structure is $15,000 per month per dealer.

The goal of Version 1 is to produce three to five months of real
campaign data that answers two questions:

  1. Do the prediction scores actually correlate with real buyer
     behavior? Are the customers scored above 0.70 buying at a
     meaningfully higher rate than those scored below it?

  2. Does direct mail with QR attribution produce a measurable,
     attributable return on investment at the campaign level?

When the answer to both questions is yes -- which the system is
designed to demonstrate -- Version 2 automation and Version 3 scale
become straightforward investments.
